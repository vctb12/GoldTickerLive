Gold Market in Dubai

Dubai is one of the world's most significant gold trading hubs. The city handles a substantial portion of global physical gold flows, driven by its strategic location, low import duties, and the cultural significance of gold across GCC and South Asian communities. Gold is sold across a wide range of venues — from the iconic traditional souks to modern mall-based retailers.

Dubai gold prices track the global XAU/USD spot rate, converted at the UAE Dirham's fixed peg of 3.6725 AED per USD. This peg has been in place since 1997, which means Dubai gold prices move in direct proportion to international spot movements — there is no currency fluctuation risk relative to USD.

22 karat gold is the dominant standard for jewellery in Dubai. 24 karat is used for investment bars and coins. 21 karat and 18 karat are widely available for contemporary and fine jewellery styles.

Key Gold Markets in Dubai

Dubai's gold trade is spread across traditional souks, purpose-built gold centres, and mall-based retailers.

Iconic Souk
Dubai Gold Souk — Deira
The most famous gold market in the Arab world. Hundreds of retailers across covered alleyways in Deira. Open daily; best visited in the evening. 22K jewellery, bullion, and coins available.
Gold Centre
Gold & Diamond Park — Al Quoz
Purpose-built retail park on Sheikh Zayed Road. Mix of established brands and independents. Contemporary and traditional designs. Good for comparison shopping in a single complex.
Mall Retail
Mall of the Emirates / Dubai Mall
Major branded jewellery chains (Damas, Malabar, Joy Alukkas, Tanishq, etc.) with fixed prices. Less negotiation scope than souks but air-conditioned and convenient.
Traditional
Al Fahidi / Bur Dubai Area
Older trading area south of the creek, with a mix of Indian, Iranian, and local gold merchants. Less tourist-oriented than the Gold Souk, often with competitive pricing.

Dubai Gold-Buying Areas

Different parts of the city have distinct gold-buying characters.

Deira (Gold Souk)
Highest concentration; traditional negotiated pricing; strongest for 22K jewellery and bullion
Bur Dubai / Al Fahidi
Mix of South Asian and Arab traders; good for Indian-style jewellery and competitive rates
Al Quoz (Gold & Diamond Park)
Modern complex; fixed and negotiated prices; contemporary designs; custom orders available
Downtown / Dubai Mall
Branded retailers; fixed prices; high footfall; less negotiation but assured brand quality
Jumeirah / Marina
Boutique and branded jewellers; higher-end designs; 18K and diamond-set pieces
Karama / Meena Bazaar
Mid-range market; popular with South Asian expats; traditional designs at accessible prices

Practical Buying Tips for Dubai

  • Check the daily gold rate board displayed at most souk shops before negotiating — the rate is typically the 22K or 24K per-gram price in AED for that session.
  • Understand making charges: the gold rate covers raw metal cost; making charges (craftwork fees, typically 3–25 AED/gram) are added on top. These are negotiable at traditional souk shops.
  • Use our Gold Calculator before you shop to know the spot-based metal value of any piece by weight and karat.
  • Dubai gold jewellery is hallmarked by the Dubai Central Laboratories. The UAE follows mandatory hallmarking — check for the hallmark stamp indicating karat purity.
  • BIS or DMCC-certified bullion bars and coins sold in Dubai can be verified against international standards. Ask for certification documents when buying investment gold.
  • VAT in the UAE is 5%, but gold bars and coins for investment purposes are zero-rated. Jewellery purchases are subject to 5% VAT. Factor this into your cost comparison.
  • Tourists can claim VAT refunds on eligible purchases through the Planet/Global Blue system at Dubai Airport upon departure.
