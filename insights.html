Understanding the Dubai Gold Souk Price vs. International Spot Price

March 10, 2026 · 5 min read · Buying Guide

If you have ever walked through the glittering corridors of Dubai's Gold Souk in Deira and then checked an international gold price app, you may have noticed a gap between what you see on screen and what the jewellers are actually charging. That gap is not a scam — it is the result of several legitimate, well-understood components layered on top of the raw market price.

What is the Spot Price (XAU/USD)?

The "spot price" of gold is the real-time market price for one troy ounce (31.1035 grams) of pure 24K gold, expressed in US Dollars. It is determined continuously on global commodities markets — principally on COMEX in New York and through the London Bullion Market Association (LBMA), which publishes a twice-daily benchmark known as the London Gold Fix. This is the number you see on Bloomberg, Reuters, or on this tracker. It represents the wholesale price for large institutional trades — typically in 400-troy-ounce bars.

What is the Dubai Gold Souk (DGJG) Daily Rate?

The Dubai Gold & Jewellery Group (DGJG) publishes a daily gold rate every morning, specific to the UAE market. This rate is expressed in AED per gram for different karat grades (24K, 22K, 21K, 18K). It is derived from the overnight LBMA closing price, converted to AED using the official peg (3.6725 AED/USD), and published before the Souk opens. All licensed jewellers in the Dubai Gold Souk are required to price their gold per gram at or above this daily rate — you will see it displayed on boards throughout the market. However, the DGJG rate is the floor, not the full price you will pay.

Why Do They Differ? Making Charges, VAT, and Dealer Margins

On top of the DGJG base rate, jewellers add three further components. First, making charges (also called "fabrication" or "labour" charges): these cover the cost of crafting the jewellery — stone-setting, polishing, design complexity, and brand premium. In the Dubai Gold Souk, making charges for machine-made 22K jewellery typically range from AED 3 to AED 12 per gram. Handmade, intricate, or designer pieces can command AED 15–30 per gram or more. Second, Value Added Tax (VAT): the UAE introduced VAT at 5% in January 2018. This applies to the total selling price including making charges. Third, a dealer margin: even the DGJG daily rate itself includes a small premium over the raw international spot price to account for the cost of importing, refining, and risk management.

How to Use Our Tracker as Your Reference Point

The price shown on this site is the bullion-equivalent price — the mathematical equivalent of what pure gold of each karat grade is worth at the current international spot price, converted to AED at the fixed 3.6725 peg. Think of it as the theoretical minimum floor, before any retail premium. When you visit the Gold Souk, compare our per-gram price for the relevant karat against the DGJG board rate: they should be within a few dirhams of each other. If the jeweller's base rate is significantly higher than our figure, the market may have moved since the DGJG morning fix — or the jeweller may be using a slightly different rate.

What is a "Fair" Making Charge?

For everyday 22K machine-made jewellery (chains, bangles, simple rings), AED 3–8 per gram is considered competitive in the Dubai Gold Souk. For hallmarked 22K pieces with moderate craftsmanship, AED 8–15 is reasonable. If a jeweller quotes you more than AED 20 per gram for plain 22K jewellery, it is worth asking whether the craftsmanship justifies the premium — or shopping around. Remember: making charges are negotiable in the Souk, especially for larger purchases. The gold price itself is fixed by the DGJG rate, but the making charge is where you have room to negotiate.
